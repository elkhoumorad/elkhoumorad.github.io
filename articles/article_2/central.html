Classical Central Potential: Orbital Dynamics

We analyze the motion of a particle with mass $m$ in a central potential field $V(r) = -k/r$. We define the angular momentum $L$ and the Hamiltonian $H$[cite: 59, 60]:

\begin{equation} L = mrv \label{eq:cp_1} \end{equation} \begin{equation} H = \frac{p^2}{2m} - \frac{k}{r} \label{eq:cp_2} \end{equation}

The velocity and momentum relationship is established via the Lagrangian[cite: 61, 62]:

\begin{equation} \frac{\partial L}{\partial v} = mv = p \implies H = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{k}{r} \label{eq:cp_3} \end{equation}

1. Kinematics in Polar Coordinates

Expressing position $\vec{r}$, velocity $\vec{v}$, and acceleration $\vec{a}$ in polar unit vectors[cite: 65, 66, 67]:

\begin{equation} \vec{r} = r\hat{e}_r \end{equation} \begin{equation} \vec{v} = \dot{r}\hat{e}_r + r\dot{\theta}\hat{e}_\theta \end{equation} \begin{equation} \vec{a} = (\ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2)\hat{e}_r + (2\dot{r}\dot{\theta} + r\ddot{\theta})\hat{e}_\theta \end{equation}

2. The Equation of the Orbit

Using the conservation of angular momentum $\dot{\theta} = L/mr^2$ [cite: 69], the radial acceleration becomes[cite: 68, 70]:

\begin{equation} \ddot{r} = \frac{L^2}{m^2r^3} - \frac{k}{mr^2} \end{equation}

Substituting $u = 1/r$ [cite: 72], we arrive at the differential equation for the orbit[cite: 74]:

\begin{equation} \frac{d^2u}{d\theta^2} + u = \frac{mk}{L^2} \end{equation}

The general solution for the path $r(\theta)$ is[cite: 75, 81]:

\begin{equation} r(\theta) = \frac{L^2/mk}{1 + \varepsilon\cos\theta} \end{equation}

Where the eccentricity $\varepsilon$ is related to the energy $E$[cite: 79, 80].

3. Energy States

Finally, we relate the system energy to the fundamental constants[cite: 83, 84]:

\begin{equation} E = -\frac{1}{n^2} \frac{me^4}{32\pi^2\epsilon_0^2\hbar^2} (1 - \varepsilon^2) \end{equation}
