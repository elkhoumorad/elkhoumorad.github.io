Hydrogen Atom Wave Function in Polar Coordinates: The Whittaker Approach

By Morad | Research Field: Semiconductor Physics

Standard approaches to the Hydrogen atom often leap directly to Laguerre polynomials. Here, we maintain mathematical rigor by utilizing the Whittaker differential equation to solve the radial component.

We begin with the time-independent Schrödinger equation:

$$\left( -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\Delta - \frac{e^2}{4\pi\varepsilon r} \right)\psi = E\psi$$

1. Radial Separation

Isolating the radial part $\psi_r$ and including the centrifugal term[cite: 1]:

$$\frac{\partial^2\psi_r}{\partial r^2} + \frac{2}{r}\frac{\partial\psi_r}{\partial r} + \left[ \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} - \frac{l(l+1)}{r^2} + \frac{2me^2}{4\pi\varepsilon\hbar^2 r} \right]\psi_r = 0$$

2. Transformation to Whittaker Form

By defining the dimensionless variable $\rho$ and constants $k$ and $\beta$:

$$\beta^2 = -4k^2 \implies \beta = 2k \implies k = \frac{1}{na}$$

The equation transforms into the Whittaker form[cite: 1]:

$$\frac{\partial^2\varphi}{\partial \rho^2} + \left[ -\frac{1}{4} + \frac{n}{\rho} - \frac{l(l+1)}{\rho^2} \right]\varphi = 0$$

3. The Total Normalized Wavefunction

Using the Whittaker function $M_{n, l+1/2}$, we define the radial normalization constant $A$:

$$A = \left( \frac{2}{na_0} \int_0^{+\infty} M_{n, l+1/2}(2\rho) d\rho \right)^{-1}$$

The total wavefunction $\psi_{n,l,m,\alpha}$ including the spherical harmonics and the pauli spinor $\alpha$ is then:

$$\psi_{n,l,m,\alpha}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{r} A \cdot M_{n, l+1/2}\left( \frac{2r}{na} \right) Y_{lm}(\theta, \phi) \alpha$$

Where the angular momentum coupling is defined as:

$$\tau = l \pm s \implies |l m_l \otimes s m_s\rangle = \sum_{\tau, m_\tau} C_{l m_l s m_s}^{\tau m_\tau} |\tau m_\tau\rangle$$
